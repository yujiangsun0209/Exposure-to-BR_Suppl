{% blocktrans trimmed %} In this task, each of you will be paired with another different player as your counterpart. You will not be told who your counterpart is either during or after the experiment, and also, your counterpart will not be told who you are either during or after the experiment. One of you will be randomly assigned as Participant 1, and the other as Participant 2. Before making a decision, you will learn your role. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans trimmed with endowment=Constants.endowment %} The task is conducted as follows: an additional wealth of {{endowment}} has been provisionally allocated to each pair of players, and Participant 1 can decide how to split these {{endowment}} between herself or himself and Participant 2. To do this, Participant 1 must decide how much she or he would like to send to Participant 2. {% endblocktrans %}

{% if subsession.treatment == Constants.one_sided_dictator %} {% blocktrans trimmed with magnitude_BR=Constants.magnitude_BR %} Once Participant 1 makes her or his decision, the server will roll a fair die (i.e., hexahedron). The result of this die-rolling will only affect Participant 1's payoff from Part 1 (not Participant 2's).
To be specific, if the die results in an odd number (i.e., 1 or 3 or 5), then Participant 1 will lose {{ magnitude_BR }}; However, if the die results in an even number (i.e., 2 or 4 or 6), then Participant 1 will gain {{ magnitude_BR }}. {% endblocktrans %} {% elif subsession.treatment == Constants.one_sided_recipient %} {% blocktrans trimmed with magnitude_BR=Constants.magnitude_BR %} Once Participant 1 makes her or his decision, the server will roll a fair die (i.e., hexahedron). The result of this die-rolling will only affect Participant 2's payoff from Part 1 (not Participant 1's).
To be specific, if the die results in an odd number (i.e., 1 or 3 or 5), then Participant 2 will lose {{ magnitude_BR }}; However, if the die results in an even number (i.e., 2 or 4 or 6), then Participant 2 will gain {{ magnitude_BR }}. {% endblocktrans %} {% elif subsession.treatment == Constants.common_sym %} {% blocktrans trimmed with magnitude_BR=Constants.magnitude_BR %} Once Participant 1 makes her or his decision, the server will roll a fair die (i.e., hexahedron). The result of this die-rolling will affect both Participant 1 and 2's payoffs from Part 1.
To be specific, if the die results in an odd number (i.e., 1 or 3 or 5), then both Participant 1 and 2 will lose {{ magnitude_BR }}; However, if the die results in an even number (i.e., 2 or 4 or 6), then both Participant 1 and 2 will gain {{ magnitude_BR }}. {% endblocktrans %} {% elif subsession.treatment == Constants.common_indep %} {% blocktrans trimmed with endowment=Constants.endowment %} Once Participant 1 makes her or his decision, the server will separately roll two fair dice (i.e., hexahedrons). The result of this dice-rolling will correspondingly affect Participant 1 and 2's payoffs from Part 1.
To be specific, the result of the first die, call it "Die 1", affects Participant 1's payoff from Part 1, such that if Die 1 results in an odd number (i.e., 1 or 3 or 5), then Participant 1 will lose {{ endowment }}; However, if Die 1 results in an even number (i.e., 2 or 4 or 6), then Participant 1 will gain {{ endowment }}.
Similarly, the result of the second die, call it "Die 2", affects Participant 2's payoff from Part 1, such that if Die 2 results in an odd number, then Participant 2 will lose {{ endowment }}; However, if Die 2 results in an even number, then Participant 2 will gain {{ endowment }}. {% endblocktrans %} {% endif %}
